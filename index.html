Tools and Technologies

What do Architects use?

Industry Tools

Drawings

Drawings help us to see how a building looks and measures.

Two Main Types of Drawings are used : Plans & Sections

Plans

A 2D representation of a building that has been cut horizontally above and parallel to the ground plane.
The viewer looks straight down at the floor and the walls.

Sample Plan

Sections

A 2D representation of a building that is cut vertically to show the main volumes of spaces.

The viewer looks straight ahead at the space and the walls.

Sample Section

Architectural Analysis

How to Analyze Architecure?

Architecure is analyzed through different lenses.

Some of the first questions I ask myself are:

How does this project bring people together?

What is the psychological reaction to this project?

How does the technology used compared to the technology available?

Design is driven by Architectural Contexts.

Context is the lens we view the project through.

For Me, the 3 most important are:

Psychological

Social

Physical

Psychological Context

This is always the first context of my analysis.

Helps analyze the factors relating to mental and emotional state of a person

Questions to ask:

What is my emotional reaction to this place?

Does this place improve mental health?

What changes would promote better mental health?

Social Context

Highly important context for public places.

Relates to the interaction of society and human beings in general.

Questions to ask:

Does this place bring people together?
If Yes, then how?
If No, then why not?

What role does this space play in the
surrounding community?

Who were the intended users of this space?

Physical Context

Vital for all places especially during Site Selection.

Analysis of the entire built and natural environments surrounding the place.

Questions to ask:

How does the architectural design respond to the site's topography and features?

Does the purpose and usage
of the space align with the chosen location?

Does the architecture contribute to the identity of the surrounding urban or natural environment?

Key Perspectives

What else is important?

Other perspectives must also be considered.

We have to consider that our architecure is used by people with variously different abilities.

We must also consider that it will be used by future generations.

Thus, we consider 1 such key perspective:

Sustainability

Which is an attempt to invent processes and outcomes that make the construction and operations of buildings viable on a long-term basis and into the future.

Currently, the industry contains lots of practices which are not sustainable.

The industry accounts for 39% of the world's total carbon dioxide emissions.

Some reasons are:

Carbon-Footprint of material gathering & transportation.

Spaces mostly accessible by vehicle and low walking access.

Destruction of the environment to allow for construction.

How can we counter this?

Through programs such as LEED.

The Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design is a rating based system that encourages and certifies sustainable practices and construction.



Takeaways

Architectural education allows us to be critical.
